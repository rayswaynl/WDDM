# Doctrine + real-kit research findings (session 6) — condensed, with layout/sprite implications

Source basis: US Army FMs via globalsecurity.org transcriptions (FM 5-103 Survivability, FM 6-50 Cannon Battery, FM 100-2-1 / 100-2-3 Soviet Army, FM 7-8 / 7-91, ATP 3-37.34). Treat as faithful transcriptions, not primary PDFs.

## TOP CORRECTIONS to my old layouts (these drive the rework)
1. **MGs belong on the FLANKS angled INWARD** (grazing/enfilade along a Final Protective Line), NOT centered firing forward. Both sides. My current emplacements have some of this but heavy variants center MGs — fix.
2. **AT fires at vehicle FLANKS** — offset to a flank with oblique lane + standoff (TOW arming ~65 m), never down the enemy axis. My layouts have TOW central/forward — fix to flank.
3. **NATO vs WarPac = degree not kind.** Soviet also disperses; portray as DENSER + more GEOMETRIC, not careless bunching.
4. **Artillery trail geometry is THE faction tell:** NATO M119 = 2 split trails in a rearward **V**; Soviet D-30 = **3 trails at 120° on a central pivot, 360°** → circular emplacement. Huge authentic differentiator.
5. **Ammo always in SEPARATE revetted pits**, set back/flank, never at the gun (sympathetic detonation). My layouts put ammo crates beside guns — move them back.
6. **NATO artillery = "lazy W"** (irregular stagger perpendicular to firing azimuth); Soviet = regular line/arc or D-30 circle.
7. **AA signature shape = CIRCULAR sandbag ring** (distinct from artillery horseshoe). MANPADS = shallow scrape/ring. Need unobstructed sky (no overhead). Soviet AA denser/layered (mix gun + MANPADS).

## Per-type LAYOUT IMPLICATIONS (→ for ~12 m top-down compositions)
- **Artillery:** NATO = 2-4 guns staggered irregularly (lazy-W: ±3-4 m lateral AND 1-2 m fore/aft), horseshoe parapet opening rear, ammo pit set back/flank. Soviet = tighter arc/line (~3 m) or D-30 circle, can show 3-leg splay, ammo central/ringed.
- **AA:** circular rings per weapon (the signature). NATO = 2 dispersed rings + MANPADS scrapes + camo, spread. Soviet = 2-4 rings clustered, mix twin-gun + MANPADS, denser.
- **Emplacement:** MGs on left+right edges angled inward (crossfire). AT offset to a flank, set back for standoff, oblique lane. Horseshoe/U parapets opening rear. NATO = staggered depth + camo + alt-position hint. Soviet = more linear, sandbag/trench line connecting, tighter.
- **Checkpoint/VCP:** linear sequence — warning/cones → S-chicane (2-3 offset blocks forcing weave) → search area → overwatch MG with clear lane down approach (elevated/sandbag), guard tower. NATO = cones + jersey/HESCO, expeditionary. Soviet/Eastern = more concrete, 2nd watchtower, boom/blocky, permanent.

## Per-system SPRITE NOTES (real-world top-down; NOT Bohemia models — real kit, original art)
WEST/US:
- **M2 Browning /M3 tripod:** dark triangular tripod (apex rear), long thin barrel forward past front edge. Gunner at rear apex.
- **Stinger (static pod):** boxy rectangular missile-pod block on small circular base, 2-4 tube mouths up/forward. (man-portable tube reads poorly top-down → use pod.)
- **TOW /M220:** tripod + thick stubby launch tube forward + small boxy sight beside it. Chunkier than MG.
- **M119 105mm:** long forward barrel + TWO trail legs splayed rear in a V + two side wheels → arrow/anchor shape.
- **M252 81 mortar:** circular baseplate + short central tube circle + small bipod V. Round, compact.
- **MK19:** like M2 but fatter squarer body, shorter stubbier barrel.
- **M2HD:** as M2, lower/flatter.
EAST/Soviet (Soviet green vs US olive/tan):
- **DShKM /wheeled:** long barrel forward + flat rectangular gun-shield + two small wheels at base sides (wheels are the tell).
- **ZU-23-2:** TWO parallel barrels forward, boxy central seat, two wheels, trail rear. Wider, twin-barrel.
- **Igla (pod):** single thick (or 2-) tube on small tripod/base, up/forward. Lighter than ZU-23.
- **D-30 122mm:** THREE legs splayed Y at 120° + long barrel over one leg + central round jack. Three-leg star = unmistakable.
- **2B14 82 mortar:** as M252 — round baseplate + bipod V.
- **Metis ATGM:** small tripod + thin launch tube forward + small control box. Compact, < TOW.
- **SPG-9:** long slender tube on low tripod, very linear, thinner than howitzers.
FORTIFICATION (plan view):
- HESCO = row of square cells; sandbag = staggered oval bags thick line (tan).
- Czech hedgehog = 6-point asterisk of crossed bars (steel/rust).
- Razor wire = line of overlapping circles (coil).
- Watchtower = square + 4 corner posts + roof square.
- Jersey barrier = rounded long bar (grey); dragon's teeth = grid of small pyramids.
